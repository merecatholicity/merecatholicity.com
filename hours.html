The Hours

The daily Psalter is the first-millennium layman’s foundation.

We hold that the Divine Office, the Church’s daily round of psalms and prayer at fixed hours, is the ordinary way a Christian sanctifies time. It is older than the monasteries that perfected it. The Psalter was the prayer book of the Lord Himself, and praying it through, week by week, was the first-millennium layman’s foundation.

Any of its forms will serve. The Monastic Diurnal, the older Breviary, the Liturgy of the Hours, or the Orthodox Horologion. The counsel for beginning is old and simple. Do not attempt everything. Take one hour, Prime in the morning or Compline before bed, pray it every day until it is a habit, and add more only when the habit holds. Consistency is worth more than volume.

The Holy Rule of St. Benedict, chapters 8 to 19. The oldest surviving ordering of the hours in the West, with the whole Psalter appointed week by week.
