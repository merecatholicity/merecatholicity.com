The Eucharist

The Mass or Divine Liturgy is the center of the week, and the rest of the rule follows from it.

We hold that the Eucharist is the source and the summit of the Christian week. The Lord commanded it, the apostles kept it, and from the first century the Church has gathered on the first day of the week to do what He said. Weekly attendance is the floor, not the ceiling. The rest of the rule of prayer exists to carry the altar’s grace into the days between.

This is not a medieval accretion. The earliest descriptions of Christian worship we possess are descriptions of exactly this, the Scriptures read, the prayers offered, the bread and cup consecrated and received. Read them and you will recognize your own parish’s Sunday.

The First Apology, by St. Justin Martyr, chapters 65 to 67. The earliest description of the Sunday liturgy, written to the emperor about the year 155.
