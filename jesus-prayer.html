The Jesus Prayer

Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.

We hold the Jesus Prayer as the shared contemplative treasure of East and West. It is the publican’s prayer of the Gospel, God be merciful to me a sinner, joined to the holy Name, and it answers the apostle’s charge to pray without ceasing. The desert fathers prayed this way from the fourth century, the East never stopped, and Cassian carried the same method West a generation later.

Pray with the breath. Find a quiet place, close your eyes, and pray the words with your breathing. Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God on the inhale. Have mercy on me, a sinner on the exhale.

Set a daily rhythm. Set aside ten or fifteen minutes each day for the prayer alone. A prayer rope, the Eastern chotki, helps keep count and keep attention.

Pray freely. Then let the prayer loose in the day. Say it quietly while driving, walking, washing dishes, and it begins to say itself. The breath and the rope are helps, not requirements. There is no technique the prayer depends on. Say the words and mean them.

The Conferences on Prayer, by St. John Cassian. The two conferences of Abbot Isaac, which taught the West unceasing prayer by a short formula, the twin of the Jesus Prayer.
