Confession and fasting

Regular penance, and the Ember days, Lent, and the ancient fasts.

We hold that regular confession and regular fasting are ordinary parts of a Christian life, not heroics for the few. Confession belongs within your own communion, honest and unspectacular, to any priest who hears you well, at whatever rhythm your confessor counsels. The pattern of fasting is early. The first-century Church already fasted twice a week, on Wednesday and Friday, and the great fast of Lent has been universal since the fourth century, with the Ember days marking the seasons in the West.

The counsel is the same as for the Hours. Do not attempt everything at once, and follow the discipline of your own communion rather than inventing one. A small fast kept is worth more than a great fast abandoned.
