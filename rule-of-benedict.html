A rule and a rhythm

The Rule of St. Benedict and the life of an oblate.

We hold up the Rule of St. Benedict as the most tested pattern of ordered Christian life the West possesses, written before the schisms and revered East and West alike. It is short, it is moderate by design, and its famous promise is kept, nothing harsh, nothing burdensome. Its three gifts to a layman are stability, the vow against endless churn, the Office, and lectio, the daily reading this page has already commended.

A layman takes the Rule up in two ways. Read it, a chapter a day as the monasteries do, and let its rhythm shape the household. Or go further and become an oblate, a layman formally attached to a monastery, keeping a rule of prayer in the world. Most Benedictine abbeys receive oblates, and the abbey nearest you is the place to ask.
